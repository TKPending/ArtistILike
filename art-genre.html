Get to know art genres

Abstract

Abstract art is a style of visual art that does not attempt to represent an accurate depiction of visual reality. Instead, it emphasizes shapes, forms, colors, and gestural marks to achieve its effect.

Portrait

Portrait art is a genre that focuses on capturing the likeness, personality, and character of an individual or a group of people through visual representation.

Landscape

Landscape art is a genre that focuses on depicting natural scenery, encompassing a wide range of outdoor environments such as mountains, valleys, rivers, forests, and other elements of the natural world.

Digital

Digital art is a broad and versatile genre that encompasses artworks created using digital technologies and tools

Realism

Realism is an artistic and literary movement that aimed to depict everyday life, society, and the natural world with a focus on accuracy, objectivity, and truthfulness

Surrealism

Surrealism is an artistic and literary movement that emerged in the early 20th century, characterized by its exploration of the irrational, dreamlike, and subconscious aspects of the human mind

Pop

Pop art is a modern art movement that celebrates the vibrant and dynamic aspects of popular culture. Characterized by its bold use of color, incorporation of everyday objects, and a playful approach to mass media, pop art blurs the lines between traditional notions of fine art and the ordinary.

Graffiti

Graffiti art is a form of visual expression rooted in urban environments, characterized by its bold and often elaborate lettering, symbols, and images sprayed or painted on public spaces.

Contemporary

Contemporary art refers to the diverse and dynamic range of artistic expressions created in the present era. Unlike specific movements or styles, contemporary art is characterized by its openness to experimentation, innovation, and exploration of various mediums and concepts.

Romanticism

Romanticism explores themes of passion, nostalgia, and the sublime, celebrating the mysterious and the unattainable. Unlike the precise realism of earlier periods, romantic artworks tend to be more subjective and personal, reflecting the artist's unique perspective on the world

Cubism

Cubism depict subjects from multiple angles simultaneously. Forms are broken down into geometric shapes and rearranged in an abstract manner, challenging the viewer's perception and inviting a new way of seeing
